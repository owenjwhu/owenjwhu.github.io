My Story

Basic Info

I earned a BA in Advertising at NJU in 2020, and I'm now a graduate student in Journalism and Communication at NJU.

My advisor is Prof. Nainan Wen (PhD, Nanyang Technological University). I also go to Prof. Chengjun Wang's (PhD, CityU of Hong Kong) reading seminar quite often. Besides, Prof. Yuxiao Wu (PhD, Northwestern University) has also provided me a lot of help since I took his social stratification class. Their help means a lot to me.

Background and Interest
I grew up in a Chinese working-class family in a small southeast town--Liuzhou, to be an adventurous boy. Growing up, I experienced so much "vigorous communication" that characterizes industrial workers' life everyday that it has been a central concern in my later life. Later, I developed interests in CMC/social media/algorithms/digital intimacy.

My research ask two questions:
(1) How do new media technologies change social relationships (romantic, friendships, family, and even random encounters)?
(2) What are the implications in the health context?


Research methods

I mostly use quantitative methods. I received great quantitative training at Nanjing University, commanding survey, experiment, computational methods, and statistical solutions for causal inferences (e.g., propensity score matching). That said, I'm also good at doing field observations, recruiting informants, and doing interviews, as I've been self-training how to exchange information and trust with people with different backgrounds all the way from my lovely family in the remote town to one of the top universities in China.

Language

I'm holding national English translator and interpreter certificate and speak fluent English (IELTS 8). I also passed German test (A1) in a month by self-taught with 94 out of 100 points. I speak Mandarin (mother tongue) and a little bit of Cantonese as well.

My Story

Basic Info

I'm now a doctoral student in Communication at Michigan State University. I earned a BA in Advertising and MA in Journalism and Communication at Nanjing University, China, in 2020 and 2022 respective. 

My master's advisor was Prof. Nainan Wen (PhD, Nanyang Technological University). I also went to Prof. Chengjun Wang's (PhD, CityU of Hong Kong) reading seminar quite often. Besides, Prof. Yuxiao Wu (PhD, Northwestern University) also provided me a lot of help since I took his social stratification class. Their help means a lot to me.

Background and Interest
I grew up in a Chinese working-class family in a small southeast town--Liuzhou, to be an adventurous boy. Growing up, I experienced so much "vigorous communication" that characterizes industrial workers' life everyday that it has been a central concern in my later life. Later, I developed interests in social media/algorithms/AI/digital intimacy/well-being.

Currently, my research asks one question:

Does the part-taking of non-human agents, which serve as both the channel and source of information transmission, in the human communication process alter the communication means by which people satiate their social needs?

Research methods

I mostly use quantitative methods. I received abundant quantitative training at Nanjing University, commanding the survey, experiment, and computational methods and statistical solutions for causal inferences (e.g., propensity score matching). That said, I'm also good at doing field observations, recruiting informants, and doing interviews, as I've been self-training on how to win trust with people from different backgrounds because of the multiple identities I have, a first-generation college student, a top university student, a minority in many aspects, etc.

Language

I'm holding national English translator and interpreter certificates and speak fluent English (IELTS 8). I also passed German test (A1) in a month by self-taught with 94 out of 100 points. I speak Mandarin (mother tongue) and a little bit of Cantonese as well.
